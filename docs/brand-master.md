# Nigel's Kitchen Table — Brand Master

This document is the single source of truth for the Nigel's Kitchen Table brand, website presentation and future business development.

## 1. Brand architecture

- **Public-facing brand:** Nigel's Kitchen Table
- **Website domain:** `thegourmetlarder.com`
- **Legacy brand role:** The Gourmet Larder remains relevant only where needed for domain continuity, existing social URLs, historic recognition and SEO. It must not compete with Nigel's Kitchen Table in prominent website copy.
- **Brand signature:** Seasonal recipes, beautiful bakes and practical kitchen knowledge, shared from Nigel's kitchen table.

## 2. Positioning

Nigel's Kitchen Table is a warm, editorial food destination for home cooks and bakers who value reliable recipes, thoughtful presentation and practical guidance.

The brand should feel more personal than a recipe database, more useful than a lifestyle mood board and less formal than a professional cookery school.

### Brand promise

Every recipe or article should help the reader make something genuinely delicious with confidence.

### Primary audience

- Home bakers and cooks looking for dependable, achievable recipes.
- Readers attracted to seasonal ingredients, comforting food and elegant presentation.
- Beginners who appreciate clear guidance and experienced cooks who value trustworthy details.

## 3. Brand personality

- Warm and welcoming
- Calm and confident
- Knowledgeable without being pretentious
- Elegant without feeling exclusive
- Personal without being personality-led
- Practical, generous and trustworthy

Nigel's portrait must remain small and secondary. Food photography, recipes and the kitchen-table atmosphere lead the visual identity.

## 4. Final visual system

### Canonical palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Ivory | `#F8F5EF` | Main page and editor background |
| Porcelain | `#FDFCF9` | Cards, forms and light surfaces |
| Linen | `#EFE9DF` | Borders, separators and quiet panels |
| Soft sage | `#A7B397` | Secondary backgrounds and subtle accents |
| Olive | `#66785F` | Primary brand colour, botanical marks and selected states |
| Forest olive | `#465443` | Primary buttons, footer and high-contrast brand areas |
| Copper | `#9A6B46` | Dividers, links, labels and restrained highlights |
| Charcoal | `#2E2E2B` | Primary text and logo lettering |
| Muted grey | `#66635D` | Secondary text and supporting details |

Olive and ivory should dominate. Copper is an accent, not a large background colour. Avoid bright greens, saturated orange, pure black, cold grey backgrounds and unapproved colour substitutions.

### Typography

- **Cormorant Garamond:** page titles, recipe titles and major section headings.
- **Source Sans 3:** body copy, navigation, labels, buttons, forms and metadata.
- **Instrument Serif:** introductions, pull quotes, Nigel's tips and occasional editorial emphasis.

Typography should feel spacious and editorial, but headings must not become so large that they reduce usability or create excessive empty space.

### Image direction

- Natural light and realistic food texture.
- Warm, restrained styling rather than high-saturation imagery.
- Consistent portrait crops for recipe cards, generally 4:5.
- Full recipe images should remain sharp and should never be stretched beyond their useful dimensions.
- Kitchen-table, ingredient and serving moments can support the food imagery.
- Avoid generic stock imagery where an authentic recipe or kitchen photograph is available.

### Logo system

- Use the horizontal Nigel's Kitchen Table logo in the desktop header.
- Use the compact logo on small screens.
- Use the NKT monogram for browser tabs, shortcuts and small social applications.
- Preserve clear space around every mark.
- Do not combine the Nigel's Kitchen Table logo with a second prominent The Gourmet Larder logo.
- Do not recolour or redraw the supplied marks outside this palette.

### Favicon and app-icon system

The bundled favicon package is part of the core identity and must ship with every theme release:

- `favicon.svg` for modern browsers.
- `favicon.ico` for legacy browser compatibility.
- `apple-touch-icon.png` for Apple home-screen shortcuts.
- `favicon-192x192.png` for Android and installable shortcuts.
- `site.webmanifest` with the approved ivory and olive colours.
- `nkt-monogram.svg` as the WordPress and admin fallback mark.

A WordPress Site Icon may override the bundled package, but it should use the approved NKT monogram rather than unrelated artwork.

## 5. Tone of voice

Copy should sound as though Nigel is calmly helping someone across the kitchen table.

### Use

- Clear British English.
- Direct instructions and useful explanation.
- Warm, natural phrasing.
- Specific details that help the reader succeed.
- First person where personal experience adds trust.

### Avoid

- Overly promotional language.
- Empty superlatives such as “the ultimate” unless demonstrably justified.
- Excessive exclamation marks.
- Corporate language, food snobbery or forced humour.
- Long personal stories before the reader receives useful recipe information.

## 6. Content pillars

### Recipes

The searchable, evergreen recipe library. Existing recipe URLs, ratings and structured data must be preserved.

Core recipe groups currently include Cakes, Biscuits & Cookies, Brownies & Bars, Bread, Pastry, Desserts & Puddings, Ice Cream and Breakfast. Taxonomy should remain useful and controlled rather than growing into many overlapping categories.

### Collections

Curated editorial groupings organised around seasons, occasions and reader needs. Examples include summer recipes, Christmas baking, afternoon tea, preserving, chocolate recipes and entertaining.

Collections support discovery, internal linking, newsletter themes, seasonal traffic and future commercial products.

### Kitchen Notes

Practical supporting knowledge, including ingredient guides, techniques, seasonal produce, equipment, preserving, fermentation and troubleshooting.

Kitchen Notes expands the business beyond individual recipes while remaining closely connected to the core promise.

## 7. Website structure

Primary navigation:

1. Recipes
2. Collections
3. Kitchen Notes
4. About Nigel
5. Contact

The homepage must remain a static page, with **Homepage: Home** selected and the **Posts page left unselected**.

### Homepage order

1. Editorial hero with one strong recipe photograph.
2. Latest recipes.
3. Seasonal collection.
4. Browse by category.
5. Popular recipes.
6. Kitchen Notes.
7. Small About Nigel section.
8. Newsletter invitation.
9. Social recipe section.
10. Branded footer.

## 8. Recipe experience

The completed branded recipe template is the benchmark for the rest of the website.

Every recipe page should provide:

- Clear title, introduction, category and useful metadata.
- Strong hero image.
- Jump to Recipe, Print and relevant sharing actions.
- Readable article width and logical heading hierarchy.
- WP Recipe Maker card styled as part of the brand rather than as an embedded third-party element.
- Nigel's tips and supporting notes using the editorial accent typeface.
- Related recipes displayed as image cards, not plain text lists.
- No obsolete sidebar clutter or duplicate newsletter forms.

## 9. Components

### Buttons

- Primary: forest olive background with porcelain text.
- Secondary: transparent or porcelain background with olive border and text.
- Text link: copper or olive with a restrained underline or arrow treatment.
- Uppercase Source Sans 3 with modest letter spacing.
- Corner radius: `5px`; avoid pill-shaped buttons.

### Cards

- Porcelain surface or open editorial layout.
- Consistent image ratios.
- Subtle linen border or very soft shadow, not both heavily.
- Category label in small uppercase text.
- Recipe title in Cormorant Garamond.
- Metadata in Source Sans 3.

### Forms

- Visible labels or accessible names.
- Porcelain fields with linen borders.
- Copper focus ring or border.
- Newsletter sections should feel intentional and should use only the configured Mailchimp form.

### Social presentation

- Keep existing social URLs until handles are deliberately changed on those platforms.
- Present **Nigel's Kitchen Table** as the visible brand on the website rather than foregrounding the legacy handle.
- Profile images should use the NKT monogram or approved wordmark.
- Social posts should use the same palette, typography hierarchy and calm editorial tone.

## 10. Business concept

Nigel's Kitchen Table begins as a trusted editorial recipe platform and grows through products that naturally extend its content.

### Foundation

- Search-friendly recipes.
- Seasonal collections.
- Kitchen Notes and practical guides.
- Newsletter audience.
- Pinterest, Instagram and other recipe discovery channels.

### Commercial development

Introduce revenue gradually and only where it supports reader trust:

1. Carefully placed advertising after traffic and layout quality justify it.
2. Affiliate links for genuinely used ingredients, equipment and books, with clear disclosure.
3. Downloadable seasonal recipe collections, baking guides and kitchen reference sheets.
4. Branded ebooks or digital cookbooks built from proven content themes.
5. Selected partnerships and sponsored content that fit the brand.
6. Courses, workshops or physical products only after audience demand is clear.

Commercial elements must never interrupt the recipe steps, obscure essential content or make the website feel like a generic affiliate site.

## 11. SEO and continuity

- Keep `thegourmetlarder.com` and all valuable existing recipe URLs.
- Do not change the permalink structure during the redesign.
- Preserve Yoast metadata, canonical URLs, XML sitemaps and recipe schema.
- Use Nigel's Kitchen Table in visible titles, descriptions and social presentation.
- Use redirects only where a page is intentionally replaced.
- Maintain The Gourmet Larder references only where operationally necessary during transition.

## 12. Brand quality test

Before publishing a page, component or marketing asset, check:

1. Does it look as though it belongs beside the completed recipe template?
2. Is the food or useful content more prominent than Nigel's portrait?
3. Are the canonical palette and type roles used consistently?
4. Is the page warm, calm and editorial rather than busy or generic?
5. Can the reader immediately understand what to do next?
6. Does the content strengthen trust and support the long-term business concept?
7. Does the browser tab, shortcut or social presentation display the approved NKT identity?
